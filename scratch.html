About the Supreme Court

The Supreme Court of the United States (SCOTUS) is the highest court in the federal judiciary of the United States. It has the ultimate authority to interpret and decide cases involving federal law, including the Constitution of the United States.

History of the Supreme Court

The Supreme Court was established in 1789 as part of the Judiciary Act of 1789. Its first session was held in New York City in 1790. Over the years, the Court's role and powers have evolved, shaping American jurisprudence.

Key moments in its history include landmark decisions such as Brown v. Board of Education (1954) which declared racial segregation in public schools unconstitutional, and Roe v. Wade (1973) which legalized abortion.

Important Supreme Court Decisions

  1. Brown v. Board of Education - Declared racial segregation in public schools unconstitutional.
  2. Roe v. Wade - Legalized abortion.
  3. Citizens United v. FEC - Struck down limits on corporate and union spending in elections.
